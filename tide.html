II. FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES OF DATA INTERACTION: MINIMALISM AND TRANSIENCE

The foundational philosophy underpinning the Application's interaction with User data is predicated upon the dual pillars of absolute necessity and deliberate impermanence. We posit that the acquisition of any User-related datum must be inextricably linked to the core, indispensable functionality of the Application â€“ namely, the provision of geographically relevant tidal information. Furthermore, any datum thus acquired must exist within the Application's purview for the briefest conceivable duration required to fulfill its designated computational role, analogous to the fleeting imprint of a wave upon the shore, extant only until the subsequent surge.

Consequently, the Application engages in the acquisition and processing of precisely one category of potentially User-associated information: Location Data. This acquisition is contingent upon the User's antecedent, affirmative, and perpetually revocable grant of permission via the standard mechanisms inherent to the Device's operating system. Absent such explicit authorization, the Application remains functionally quiescent with respect to location-specific features.

III. SCOPE AND METHODOLOGY OF LOCATION DATA UTILIZATION

The singular purpose for which the Application solicits and momentarily utilizes Location Data is the ascertainment of the User's proximate geographical position relative to established coastal reference points integral to hydrographic modeling and tidal prediction algorithms. The sequence of operations is delineated with painstaking specificity as follows:

  1. Permission Solicitation: Upon initial activation of location-dependent features, or if permission has been previously denied or revoked, the Application invokes the standard operating system interface to request the User's authorization for access to the Device's location services.
  2. Data Acquisition: Subsequent to the granting of permission, the Application receives the current geographical coordinates (latitude and longitude) as reported by the Device's integrated positioning systems (e.g., GPS, Wi-Fi triangulation, cellular network positioning). The precision level of this data may vary based on Device capabilities and environmental factors but is utilized solely to the extent necessary for identifying the relevant hydrographic region.
  3. Computational Processing: The acquired coordinates are immediately employed as input parameters for internal algorithms or calls to integrated hydrographic datasets. These algorithms correlate the provided location with the nearest relevant tidal prediction station or model grid point.
  4. Result Presentation: Based upon the correlation established in the preceding step, the Application calculates and displays the predicted tidal information (e.g., high/low tide times and heights) corresponding to the identified station or region.
  5. Data Discarding: Immediately following the successful calculation and rendering of the tidal prediction results, the acquired Location Data is expunged from the Application's active memory and operational state. It is neither written to persistent storage on the Device by the Application, nor is it transmitted, logged, aggregated, anonymized for external use, or otherwise retained within any system or infrastructure under the Proprietor's control. The interaction is strictly transactional and ephemeral.

It is imperative to underscore that the Application does not engage in background location tracking, continuous monitoring, geofencing, or the creation of historical location profiles. The acquisition of Location Data occurs solely upon User initiation of a tidal information request or refresh action, contingent upon extant permission.
